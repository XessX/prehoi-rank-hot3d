# Abstract Draft

This study investigates pre-contact forecasting of 3D hand-object interactions from egocentric observations for assistive augmented reality. The proposed Affordance-Grounded PreHOI-Former framework is designed to predict the intended target object, future interaction type, future 3D hand pose, and likely contact or affordance region before physical contact occurs. The current repository implements only a minimum viable PyTorch pipeline with synthetic tensors, a transformer baseline, and reproducible training/evaluation scaffolding. Real HOT3D experiments, affordance labels, and vision-language guidance remain planned work.
